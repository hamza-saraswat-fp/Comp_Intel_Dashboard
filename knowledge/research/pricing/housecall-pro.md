# Housecall Pro — Pricing & Packaging Profile
*Prepared for FieldPulse · July 2026*

Housecall Pro positions itself as an all-in-one field service management platform for home service businesses — plumbing, HVAC, electrical, cleaning, and similar trades — spanning solo operators up to larger multi-crew shops. Its pricing is structured as a classic three-tier SaaS ladder (Basic / Essentials / MAX) with a low-priced single-user entry point, but the practical floor for any team of 2+ is the mid tier, and Housecall Pro leans heavily on a long tail of paid, mostly custom-quoted add-ons (AI voice answering, phone/voice, GPS fleet tracking, sales proposals, lead pipeline, websites, payroll) layered on top of the base subscription. This add-on-heavy model is a frequently cited complaint in third-party reviews as a source of "cost creep." As of July 2026, Housecall Pro's core AI capabilities (Marketing AI, Analyst AI, Coach AI, Help AI, CSR AI chat) are bundled free into every plan, with 24/7 AI phone answering (CSR AI voice) sold as a separate paid add-on.

## Plans

| Plan | Monthly (billed monthly) | Monthly (billed annually) | Users | Headline feature gates |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Basic** | $79/mo | $59/mo | 1 user | Online booking, invoicing & payments, scheduling/dispatching, quotes & proposals, review management, job costing, price book, customer communication |
| **Essentials** | $189/mo | $149/mo | Up to 5 users | Everything in Basic, plus QuickBooks integration, flat-rate pricing, customer equipment tracking, photo reports & annotations, commissions, employee GPS tracking, checklists |
| **MAX** | $329/mo | $299/mo | Up to 8 users (additional users $35/mo each) | Everything in Essentials, plus advanced custom reporting, open API access, dedicated onboarding, escalated phone support, Sales Proposal Tool, Recurring Service Plans |

*(As of Jul 2026, per housecallpro.com/pricing.)* Basic is effectively a single-operator tier — adding a second user forces an upgrade to Essentials, a jump from $59/mo to $149/mo on annual billing. Several secondary sources characterize Essentials, not Basic, as the realistic "starting" price for most contractor teams (reported, unverified by vendor — framing/opinion from review sites, not a vendor claim).

## Payment processing

Per housecallpro.com/features/payment/ and housecallpro.com/features/instapay/ (as of Jul 2026):

- **Card processing:** starts at **2.59%** (standard rate; the page does not explicitly separate card-present vs. card-not-present rates — both appear to fall under this general rate).
- **American Express / commercial, corporate & business cards:** **3.49%** (reported via search snippet from Housecall Pro's help center content, not independently re-confirmed via direct fetch — treat as (reported, unverified by vendor) pending direct page confirmation).
- **ACH / bank payments:** **1%** processing fee.
- **Instapay (expedited payout):** standard card or ACH processing fee **+1%** per transaction, typically depositing funds in under 30 minutes (vs. ~2 business days standard for cards, up to 5–7 business days for ACH).
- **Check deposits (via mobile app):** **no fee**.
- **Consumer financing:** 0%–35.9% APR depending on customer creditworthiness (example terms cited: 0% APR for 3 months or 8.9% APR for 12 months on a $1,200 purchase).

## Packaging observations

- **AI is bundled, not gated, for the "brains" — but gated for the "phone."** Housecall Pro gives away four of its five AI Team tools (Marketing AI, Analyst AI, Coach AI, Help AI) free across every plan including Basic, using them as a retention/stickiness layer. Only the highest-value, cost-to-serve-intensive capability — 24/7 AI phone answering — is walled off as a paid add-on. This suggests Housecall Pro treats generative/analytical AI as a commodity table-stakes feature but treats live-call automation (which competes directly with a human hire or a paid answering service) as a premium, separately monetized product.
- **The pricing ladder is really a 2-tier decision in practice.** Basic's 1-user cap makes it a trial-adjacent/solo-operator tier; any business with a second employee is pushed to Essentials, a $90/mo (annual) jump. This is a steep single step compared to more gradual per-seat pricing models, and is called out repeatedly by third-party reviewers as a pain point.
- **Heavy reliance on "contact for pricing" add-ons.** Unlike the core plan tiers (which are transparently published), the large majority of Housecall Pro's add-on ecosystem — Voice, Pipeline, Websites, Payroll, Accounting, HCP Assist, and most AI add-ons beyond CSR AI voice — do not have public prices. This is a deliberate packaging choice that pushes prospects into a sales conversation for anything beyond the core plan, likely to enable custom/negotiated pricing and to obscure true total cost of ownership from public comparison.
- **Per-user overage pricing only exists at the top tier.** Basic and Essentials are hard-capped (no paid seat expansion); only MAX offers pay-as-you-grow seats at $35/mo/user. This funnels growing multi-crew businesses toward MAX specifically to get seat flexibility, not just for MAX's feature set.
- **Payments are a second monetization layer, not just a convenience feature.** With card rates starting at 2.59%, ACH at 1%, and an extra +1% "Instapay" fee for expedited payout, Housecall Pro Payments functions as an integrated, story-driven revenue stream (introduced via urgency: "can't wait two business days for your money") on top of the subscription — a common FSM/vertical-SaaS pattern also relevant to how FieldPulse should think about payments monetization.
