# ServiceTitan — Pricing & Packaging Profile
*Prepared for FieldPulse · July 2026*

ServiceTitan is the enterprise/premium anchor of the field service management category — publicly traded (NASDAQ: TTAN), sales-led, and explicitly optimized for larger trades businesses rather than small shops. The company does not publish a pricing table anywhere on servicetitan.com; every tier page ends in a "Request Pricing" or "Get a Demo" call-to-action, and G2/Capterra/TrustRadius all list pricing as "not provided by vendor." Market perception, echoed consistently across review sites, dozens of independent cost-breakdown blogs, and ServiceTitan's own public statement that the platform is "not optimized for companies with 3 or fewer technicians," is that ServiceTitan is priced for and marketed to shops with roughly 20+ technicians, requires a sales demo and annual contract to get a number at all, and carries meaningfully higher total cost of ownership than self-serve competitors like Jobber or Housecall Pro once implementation fees and "Pro" add-ons are factored in.

## Plans

ServiceTitan's own pricing page (servicetitan.com/pricing) confirms three named tiers but publishes no dollar figures for any of them — every tier links to a "Request Pricing" form. The tier names and feature deltas below are vendor-confirmed; all dollar figures are secondhand estimates and are marked accordingly.

| Tier (vendor-confirmed name) | What's vendor-confirmed as included | Reported starting price (unverified by vendor) |
|---|---|---|
| **Starter** | Core scheduling, dispatching, call booking, invoicing, pricebook | **$245–$300/technician/month** (reported, unverified by vendor) |
| **Essentials** | Everything in Starter + mobile estimates, payroll management, expanded CRM/reporting/integrations | **$300–$400/technician/month** (reported, unverified by vendor) |
| **The Works** | Everything in Essentials + advanced reporting, commission tracking, customizable memberships, marketing tools | **$400–$500/technician/month** (reported, unverified by vendor) |

Pricing model: **per-technician / per-user**, layered with a platform/base fee — this per-tech structure is vendor-confirmed as the billing basis (ServiceTitan's pricing page and its Q4 FY2026 earnings call both describe pricing as "linked to techs in the field"), but the actual per-seat dollar amount is not published anywhere by ServiceTitan.

The $245–$500/tech/month range above is a convergence of secondhand reports: multiple independent sources (Projul, MyQuoteIQ, FieldCamp, ServiceTitanPricing.com, ITQlick) cite figures in this same band, sourced by those authors from G2/Capterra/TrustRadius user reviews, Reddit threads, and BBB complaint filings — not from ServiceTitan directly. Treat this as directionally reliable but **not** a verified rate card. All plans reportedly require 12+ month annual contracts (reported, unverified by vendor).

## Add-ons

ServiceTitan sells a family of "Pro" products as separately licensed add-ons on top of any base tier, plus Titan Intelligence (its AI layer) and payments/financing products. None of the prices below are published by ServiceTitan; all are marked per the sourcing discipline below.

| Add-on / module | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Dispatch Pro** | Not publicly disclosed | Vendor-confirmed to exist as a paid add-on (servicetitan.com/features/pro). Uses Titan Intelligence to auto-optimize the dispatch board using job-value predictions and technician performance data. No price found in any source, vendor or secondhand. |
| **Pricebook Pro** | **$200–$500/month** (reported, unverified by vendor) | Flat-rate pricebook management tool. |
| **Marketing Pro** | **~$2,000+/month**, alt. estimate **$500–$1,500+/month** (reported, unverified by vendor) | Email campaigns, direct mail, call tracking, ad ROI attribution. Includes an "Ads Optimizer" AI feature that feeds ServiceTitan audience/revenue data into Google Ads. Estimates vary meaningfully across sources — treat as a wide, low-confidence range. |
| **Phones Pro / Contact Center Pro** | Estimated **$300–$800+/month** (reported, unverified by vendor) | VoIP phone system with AI call transcription; "Contact Center Pro" is the broader rebrand/expansion of this line and now includes AI Voice Agents (see below) and "Manager Assist." |
| **Fleet Pro** | Not publicly disclosed | GPS tracking, geofencing, unauthorized-use alerts. Vendor-confirmed to exist; no price found anywhere. |
| **Scheduling Pro** | Not publicly disclosed | Omnichannel 24/7 online/SMS booking engine. Vendor-confirmed to exist; no price found anywhere. |
| **AI Voice Agents** (part of Contact Center Pro) | **Per-call usage fee**, exact rate not publicly disclosed | Vendor materials describe this as usage-based ("no commitment required") rather than flat-fee, but no per-call dollar rate was found in any source. |
| **Titan Intelligence (TI)** | Not a separately priced SKU — see packaging note below | Vendor positions TI as the underlying AI/data engine "only available in ServiceTitan," powering features embedded across the Starter/Essentials/Works tiers and the Pro line (not a standalone paid tier itself). |
| **Atlas** (AI "sidekick"/copilot, part of TI) | Not publicly disclosed | Conversational AI interface layered on top of Titan Intelligence — lets users run reports, find jobs, dispatch techs via natural language. Launched publicly ~September 2025. No standalone price found; appears bundled into the platform rather than sold separately, but this is an inference, not a vendor confirmation. |
| **Max** (agentic operating system, formerly reported as a bundle of AI capabilities) | **Priced like the core per-technician subscription** — vendor-confirmed via Q4 FY2026 earnings call, no dollar figure disclosed | CFO Dave Sherry, Q4 FY2026 earnings call: "For now, Max is a single package that's priced like our core solution based on the number of technicians generating revenue in the field... this may evolve over time." Sherry also described Virtual Agent calls as a separate **usage-based "AI consumption"** layer, "additive to Max" — implying a second, consumption-based pricing dimension on top of the per-tech Max subscription. As of the Q4 call, Max was still in a "cautious rollout" (~50 pilot customers, "hundreds more" in backlog). |
| **ServiceTitan Financing** (via Wisetack partnership) | **3.9% merchant transaction fee** on completed financed jobs (reported, unverified by vendor — sourced from Wisetack's own published merchant terms, not ServiceTitan) | Consumer installment loans, $500–$25,000, 0%–35.9% APR to the end customer. No implementation cost or subscription fee reported for the merchant beyond the per-transaction fee. |

## Payment processing

ServiceTitan sells its own integrated processor, **Titan Payments**, but does not publish a rate card. ServiceTitan's own payments FAQ states rates are customized per business based on processing volume, technology requirements, transaction environment, and risk, and directs prospects to a dedicated fintech sales email rather than a public price. One third-party payments-consulting site (merchantcostconsulting.com) estimates **markups of 0.25%–0.75% over interchange**, with **effective blended rates commonly cited around 2.9% + $0.30/transaction** for integrated card-present/card-not-present processing — this is a third-party analyst estimate, not a ServiceTitan-published rate, and should be treated as a rough industry-typical benchmark rather than a confirmed figure.

## Recent pricing changes

- **September 2025** — ServiceTitan publicly launched **Atlas**, its AI "sidekick"/conversational copilot built on Titan Intelligence, at its Pantheon customer conference. Framed as a new interface layer across the platform rather than a new paid SKU. (Source: SiliconANGLE, StockTitan coverage of the Pantheon 2025 launch.)
- **Q3 FY2026 earnings call** — Management characterized **Pro-product pricing and packaging as "not yet fully optimized,"** signaling the company itself views current Pro-line pricing structure as unsettled and likely to change. Same call reported record free cash flow ($38M, up from $11M YoY) and 26% YoY subscription revenue growth.
- **Q4 FY2026 earnings call (reported ~March 2026)** — First detailed public commentary on **Max** pricing mechanics: priced as a single package tied to technician count (same basis as core subscription), with **Virtual Agent / AI voice calls priced separately as usage-based consumption**, explicitly "additive to Max." Management also disclosed that at "full ramp," Max customers **roughly double their subscription revenue** to ServiceTitan — with report also citing ~50% higher average ticket size and 18–30% EBITDA gains for those customers — while stressing this uplift is not driven by adding more licensed technicians, implying it comes from Max/Pro/usage attach rather than seat growth. ServiceTitan also outlined a **$1.11B–$1.12B FY2027 revenue target** explicitly tied to Max ramp. (Source: Motley Fool Q4 FY2026 transcript; Seeking Alpha coverage.)
- No evidence found of any published, dated change to the base per-technician list price itself — consistent with the fact that ServiceTitan has never published one to begin with.

## Packaging observations

- **AI is being layered, not bundled flat.** Titan Intelligence functions as an underlying engine spread across existing tiers/Pro-products (dispatch optimization, price benchmarking, ad optimization) rather than sold as its own line item, while newer, more agentic capabilities (Max, Virtual Agents) are being introduced as an *additional* subscription layer plus a *separate* usage-based consumption fee — a classic "platform tax + metered AI" structure rather than a flat AI add-on price.
- **The modular "Pro" ladder is the primary post-signature upsell lever.** Five-plus separately licensed Pro modules (Dispatch, Pricebook, Marketing, Phones/Contact Center, Fleet, Scheduling) let ServiceTitan expand revenue per existing customer without changing the base tier price — multiple independent sources peg this as adding 30–50%+ to a customer's total monthly bill over time.
- **Pricing is explicitly acknowledged internally as unfinished.** Management said on the Q3 FY2026 call that Pro pricing/packaging is "not yet fully optimized" — a rare admission from a public company that its packaging strategy is still in flux, and a signal that further add-on or AI monetization changes should be expected.
- **Sales-led, quote-gated pricing is a deliberate segment filter, not an oversight.** No free trial, no public price, no self-serve signup, mandatory demo, and reportedly-required 12+ month contracts all point to a strategy of pricing out small/self-serve buyers in favor of higher-ACV, higher-touch enterprise trades accounts — reinforced by ServiceTitan's own statement that it isn't optimized for shops with 3 or fewer technicians.
- **Implementation cost is a real, well-documented barrier to entry**, independent of subscription price: reported figures cluster consistently at $5K–$15K for small deployments up to $30K–$50K+ for larger/enterprise ones, with onboarding commonly taking 3–12 months — this is one of the more consistently corroborated (if still unverified-by-vendor) figures across sources, and a meaningful competitive contrast point against FieldPulse's lower-friction onboarding.
