# Competitor Teardown — Scheduling & Dispatch
*Jobber · Housecall Pro · ServiceTitan | Prepared for FieldPulse · July 2026*

Scheduling and dispatch is the daily-use core of every FMS platform — it's where office staff spend most of their screen time and where the "does this software actually save me time" verdict gets made. This teardown documents how Jobber, Housecall Pro, and ServiceTitan implement scheduling and dispatch at the UI/workflow level: calendar and dispatch board mechanics, route optimization, technician notifications, recurring-job handling, online booking, and each vendor's emerging AI-assisted dispatch layer. Sourcing is limited to public vendor help centers, product/feature pages, and pricing pages — no competitor logins, no NDA'd material. Where help.getjobber.com blocked automated fetching (it returned 403s on several attempts), findings are corroborated from Google-indexed snippets of the same articles or from Jobber's public feature/pricing pages instead. Every claim below traces to a real source link; anything that couldn't be verified from a public page is marked "(unverified)" rather than asserted.

## 1 · Calendar & job scheduling

**Jobber.** Jobber's core scheduling surface is simply the **Schedule**, mid-migration from a "Legacy Schedule" to a redesigned "New Schedule" the help center describes as rolling out through fall 2026 (existing accounts can opt in early). The unit of work is the **Visit**: a job (the overall contract/project) can have one visit or many, and visits are what appear on the calendar. On the New Schedule, unscheduled work sits in an **Unscheduled Visits** drawer rather than a persistent side column, sortable by Oldest/Newest first, Client name, Highest/Lowest value, or manual drag-order, then dragged onto the calendar. Any appointment can be click-and-dragged to a new day or time. Standard views are Day, Week, Month, List, and Map, with color coding and filters (team member, job status) for triage. Single-visit reassignment happens by opening the appointment, selecting Edit, and changing the Team field; a "Reschedule and Reassign" tool handles bulk moves from a "More" button.
Source: [New Schedule](https://help.getjobber.com/hc/en-us/articles/29840886387351-New-Schedule) · [Schedule and dispatch efficiently](https://www.getjobber.com/features/schedule-and-dispatch-efficiently/) · [Assigning Multiple Visits at Once to Users](https://help.getjobber.com/hc/en-us/articles/115009378707-Assigning-Multiple-Visits-at-Once-to-Users-Legacy-Schedule) · [Month & Week View](https://help.getjobber.com/hc/en-us/articles/115009164267-Month-Week-View-Legacy-Schedule)

**Housecall Pro.** The **Schedule tab** is the primary calendar, reachable from Job pages, Estimate pages, and the customer intake flow, not just its own nav item. A view-mode dropdown switches between **Schedule** (default), **Dispatch** (employee columns side-by-side), **Day**, **Week**, **Monday–Friday**, **Month**, and **View by Employee** (each tech gets a column with profile photo up top, built for comparing availability). Jobs render as color-coded tiles: scheduled jobs are colored by employee or service ZIP code, in-progress/"on my way" jobs show white with a colored outline, completed-but-unpaid jobs are grey with a red outline, paid/completed jobs are solid grey, and unassigned jobs are dark grey with a light-grey avatar placeholder. Unscheduled jobs sit as draggable grey boxes in a side panel and drag onto a date/time slot; dragging an already-scheduled tile reschedules it and, if enabled on the customer's profile, can trigger an automatic reschedule notification. Two special layouts: **Anytime Jobs** pin to the calendar top rather than a time slot, and **Multi-Day Jobs** span a top row across several dates.
Source: [How to Use the Schedule Tab (Calendar)](https://help.housecallpro.com/en/articles/6367496-how-to-use-the-schedule-tab-calendar) · [Scheduling Jobs and Estimates](https://help.housecallpro.com/en/articles/6758788-scheduling-jobs-and-estimates) · [Scheduling and Calendar FAQ](https://help.housecallpro.com/en/articles/9483598-scheduling-and-calendar-faq)

**ServiceTitan.** ServiceTitan separates two calendar-shaped surfaces: the **Schedule Board**, a higher-level calendar showing booked appointments, recurring jobs, and non-job events across a month/week/three-day/day span, and the **Dispatch Board**, the operational, real-time assignment tool (Section 2). Both organize around **Business Units** — the trade/department construct (e.g., "HVAC Install Residential" vs. "HVAC Service Commercial") most enterprise accounts use to segment reporting and dispatch scope; every job booked through Scheduling Pro is tagged to a Business Unit automatically based on campaign, customer responses, job type, service zone, or trade. This Business Unit layer is the single biggest structural difference from Jobber and Housecall Pro, neither of which has an equivalent segmentation primitive in the calendar itself.
Source: [Use the Schedule Board](https://help.servicetitan.com/docs/use-the-schedule-board) · [Best practices for managing business units](https://help.servicetitan.com/docs/best-practices-for-managing-business-units) · [Setting up for success: Commercial Best Practices](https://www.servicetitan.com/commercial-playbook/services-and-departments)

## 2 · Dispatch board & technician assignment

**Jobber.** Jobber doesn't have a dedicated "Dispatch Board" as a separate screen — dispatching is an extension of the Schedule view. Team-level visibility comes from filtering the calendar by team member, and reassignment happens per-visit (open it, edit the Team field) or in bulk via Reschedule and Reassign. When a team member's day changes, Jobber sends real-time push notifications to the mobile app so field staff learn immediately about new, rescheduled, or canceled visits, plus a daily work summary before shifts start. Assignment is manual and location/availability-aware — a dispatcher can see a live map of team locations to judge who's closest — but there's no automated skill-tag filter the way ServiceTitan has; Jobber's own "Team Management" content describes assignment as "based on availability and location" rather than a formal skills taxonomy.
Source: [Team Management for Field Service Businesses](https://www.getjobber.com/features/team-management/) · [Schedule in the Jobber App](https://help.getjobber.com/hc/en-us/articles/6766253760279-Schedule-in-the-Jobber-App) · [Schedule and dispatch efficiently](https://www.getjobber.com/features/schedule-and-dispatch-efficiently/)

**Housecall Pro.** The **Dispatch view** is a distinct mode within the same Schedule tab (not a separate section) that lays technicians out as columns for a day-at-a-glance view. Assignment happens by clicking a pencil icon next to the dispatched employee name(s), checking/unchecking names (or "Unassigned"), and saving — or by dragging a job tile into a technician's column. HCP explicitly supports multiple technicians per job for crew-based work, giving a "clear view of who's scheduled where." Dispatchers can call or text a technician directly from inside the app — a lighter-weight version of ServiceTitan's Activity Center. The stated guidance is to send "the right technicians to the right jobs on time by assigning jobs based on skills, location, or availability with one tap," though the underlying skill data is informal (team member tags), not a structured skills-to-job-type mapping.
Source: [Best Field Service Dispatch Software With Real-Time GPS Map](https://www.housecallpro.com/features/dispatching-software/) · [How to Use the Schedule Tab (Calendar)](https://help.housecallpro.com/en/articles/6367496-how-to-use-the-schedule-tab-calendar) · [Manage Team Member Profiles](https://help.housecallpro.com/en/articles/2865944-manage-team-member-profiles)

**ServiceTitan.** The **Dispatch Board** is ServiceTitan's most purpose-built screen in this teardown, with **Daily**, **Weekly**, **Classic** (legacy), **Map**, and **Crews** view variants. Below the main technician-row grid sits the **Job Tray**, a tabbed panel whose default tab is **Unassigned** alongside Alerts, Scheduled, Unconfirmed, Dispatched, Working, Paused, Done, Hold, Canceled, and Future — a full job-lifecycle queue neither competitor exposes as explicitly. Assignment happens by dragging a job from Unassigned onto a technician's row, via a "Select Technician" dropdown under "Assign Technicians," or via right-click reassign. Tiles are color-coded by status (light blue = scheduled, dark blue = confirmed, green = working, purple = dispatched, yellow = paused, light gray = done, dark gray = non-job event, red outline = alert) — a denser status vocabulary than either competitor's. Once assigned, a dispatcher clicks Action → Dispatch, which pushes job details and address to the technician's mobile device. Toolbar controls include Sync, Settings, Filter (by Business Unit, team, or skill), an **Activity Center** for technician messaging, and a "Show Map" toggle. Assignment eligibility is gated by a formal **Skills** system: skills are defined under Settings > Operations > Skills and applied per-technician under Settings > People > Technicians, after which job types can require specific skills — the board then only surfaces technicians who hold them, and skill levels stack (a Level 3 tech is also tagged Level 1 and 2 so they show up for lower-tier work).
Source: [Use the Daily Dispatch Board](https://help.servicetitan.com/how-to/using-dispatch-board) · [Dispatch Board FAQ](https://help.servicetitan.com/docs/dispatch-board-faq) · [Manage skills for technicians and job types](https://help.servicetitan.com/how-to/tech-skills) · [Assigning the Right Technician](https://www.servicetitan.com/guides/contractor-playbook/technician-assignments)

## 3 · Route optimization & mapping

**Jobber.** Jobber's **Route Optimization** feature computes what it calls the most efficient and cost-effective path between visits, factoring in time of day, typical traffic, road closures, and current team member locations, then renders that route as lines on a Map view layered over the schedule. Optimization can be run automatically or triggered manually, and it works from both the vertical Day view and the Week view — meaning a dispatcher can optimize a single tech's day or restructure an entire crew's week at once ("whether you're dispatching one person or a full crew"). Once a route is set, assigned techs get turn-by-turn directions pushed to their phone along with instant notifications of route changes. Route Optimization is a paid-tier feature: it is not included on the entry **Core** plan and only unlocks starting at **Connect**/**Grow** (see pricing note in Section 6).
Source: [Top-Rated Service Routing Software](https://www.getjobber.com/features/route-optimization/) · [How to Route](https://help.getjobber.com/hc/en-us/articles/360033836293-How-to-Route) · [Daily Route Optimization](https://help.getjobber.com/hc/en-us/articles/115009542508-Daily-Route-Optimization) · [What is Route Optimization?](https://www.getjobber.com/academy/what-is-route-optimization/)

**Housecall Pro.** Housecall Pro's routing shows estimated drive time between jobs directly on the schedule/map so a dispatcher can "assign the nearest techs to new or changed appointments," and route changes made in the office sync automatically to the technician's mobile app in real time so techs stay on the updated path without a manual push. The map view in Dispatch mode lets a dispatcher preview job addresses and customer info without leaving the scheduling screen. For fleet-level tracking beyond simple job-to-job routing, Housecall Pro offers a dedicated **Vehicle GPS Tracking** feature/add-on that shows every company vehicle on a live map, flags idle time, and integrates with third-party GPS/fleet providers (e.g., Force Fleet Tracking) for extended telematics.
Source: [Best Field Service Dispatch Software With Real-Time GPS Map](https://www.housecallpro.com/features/dispatching-software/) · [Vehicle GPS Tracking: Overview](https://help.housecallpro.com/en/articles/6060351-vehicle-gps-tracking-overview) · [Track and Manage Company Vehicles with GPS Fleet Software](https://www.housecallpro.com/features/vehicle-gps-tracking/)

**ServiceTitan.** ServiceTitan splits routing into two tools at different scopes. **Optimize Technician Route** works per-technician, per-day: it only reorders jobs already Scheduled and assigned solely to that tech for that day (Confirmed and other-status jobs are left untouched), and ServiceTitan says it's most valuable for businesses running many jobs/day with flexible, wide arrival windows — the example given is pest control running 10–15 jobs/day with 8-hour windows. Board-wide optimization across multiple technicians is instead **Dispatch Pro**'s job (Section 4). Separately, the **Map** view (Map 2.0) — opened from Dispatch → Daily dropdown → Map — shows technicians as numbered, color-coded rectangles and appointments as matching-colored squares, filterable by Priority Level, Technician, Skill Level, and Tag; ServiceTitan recommends running Map 2.0 and the Dispatch Board on two screens side-by-side. A further live-fleet layer, comparable to Housecall Pro's GPS add-on, comes via the **Fleet Pro** module.
Source: [Use Optimize Technician Route](https://help.servicetitan.com/docs/use-optimize-technician-route) · [Dispatch efficiently with Map 2.0](https://help.servicetitan.com/docs/dispatch-efficiently-with-map-20) · [Dispatch & Track Your Technicians](https://help.servicetitan.com/docs/dispatch-and-track-your-technicians)

## 4 · AI-assisted dispatch & scheduling

**Jobber.** Jobber's most directly comparable AI-adjacent scheduling feature is **Find a Time**, which scans team availability and drive time to surface the best open calendar slots instead of making a dispatcher scan the whole board manually; it's available on the Month, Week, and Day views and gated to the **Grow** and **Plus** plans. Availability is computed from each employee's existing schedule assignments, calculated drive time, and configured working hours. Separately, an **AI Receptionist** (powered by Jobber AI) answers inbound calls/texts, matches the caller to an existing client profile, and can book a job directly into the calendar; follow-up tasks it creates are auto-assigned to whichever team members are listed under "task assignment" and scheduled for the same day as the call. Receptionist ships included on **Plus**, or as an add-on (public reporting cites roughly $29–$99/month depending on tier and volume) on lower plans. Jobber does not appear to publish a standalone AI *dispatch-optimization* engine comparable to Dispatch Pro — its AI investment so far weights toward intake/booking (Receptionist) and slot-finding (Find a Time), not automated technician-to-job assignment.
Source: [The #1 Service Scheduling Software in 2025](https://www.getjobber.com/features/scheduling/) · [Schedule Overview](https://help.getjobber.com/hc/en-us/articles/36628696269975-Schedule-Overview-New-Schedule) · [Receptionist–powered by Jobber AI](https://help.getjobber.com/hc/en-us/articles/25315927533847-Receptionist-powered-by-Jobber-AI) · [The power of AI in your toolbox](https://www.getjobber.com/features/ai/)

**Housecall Pro.** Housecall Pro's AI dispatch layer is **CSR AI**, part of a broader "AI Team" umbrella (also including Analyst AI for reporting and Marketing AI for content). CSR AI answers calls and chats 24/7, matches line items to a customer's stated request, checks technician availability, and books the job onto the calendar the same way a human CSR would, using the account's existing services, hours, and availability configuration. On assignment, Housecall Pro states jobs get "assigned based on skills, location, or availability with one tap," and its marketing describes AI matching incoming jobs to available techs by location, skill set, and schedule — though the public docs don't detail a scoring algorithm the way ServiceTitan's do. Most AI Team members are included free and enabled by default; **CSR AI specifically is an optional paid add-on** for 24/7 call-answering. Housecall Pro's roadmap notes mention CSR AI extending into SMS handling and Spanish-language conversation (unverified rollout timing).
Source: [CSR AI Overview](https://help.housecallpro.com/en/articles/9740104-csr-ai-overview) · [24/7 AI Customer Service Assistant for Home Services](https://www.housecallpro.com/features/ai-team/csr-ai/) · [AI Assistants for Service Businesses to Automate, Scale & Grow](https://www.housecallpro.com/features/ai-team/) · [AI Team Overview](https://help.housecallpro.com/en/articles/9311875-ai-team-overview)

**ServiceTitan.** **Dispatch Pro** is the most fully-built AI dispatch product of the three, and explicitly a paid **Pro** add-on on top of the base Dispatch Board. It runs on **Titan Intelligence** and offers two modes: an **Assist**-style mode where the system recommends a technician per job for a dispatcher to review/approve, and a full **Auto Mode** that assigns "Dispatch Pro–enabled" jobs automatically — the board reshuffles every 10 minutes during business hours (hourly off-hours) and optimizes up to 3 days forward by default (extendable to 2 years via "Additional Days to Plan"). The system applies a sequential hard-constraint filter — Dispatch Pro-enabled status, zone/Business Unit alignment, required job-type skill, shift/arrival-window availability, max drive-time, then performance ranking — then simulates "hundreds or more scenarios" per day to maximize predicted revenue, using historical technician performance, predicted job value, and drive time as inputs. Businesses can tune it to prioritize drive time, ticket size, or a blend, and cap max drive time. ServiceTitan states Dispatch Pro never overrides a human, works only with "managed technicians," needs admin setup, and improves as historical data accumulates (value is "felt over a few weeks"; positioned for businesses that "dispatch for dollars" — HVAC/plumbing/electrical). Vendor-cited results: up to 1.5x revenue growth, 1.75x average ticket growth, 2x dispatcher efficiency, 6% less drive time, 14% higher average ticket (unverified independently). Beyond dispatch, ServiceTitan's 2026 **Atlas**/**Max** AI messaging extends into demand-based capacity — the company states AI agents can detect open capacity and auto-throttle or trigger marketing to fill gaps, reporting call-booking and close-rate lifts for Max-tier accounts, though the capacity-marketing mechanics aren't detailed publicly.
Source: [Dispatch Pro | ServiceTitan](https://www.servicetitan.com/features/pro/dispatch) · [Dispatch Pro overview](https://help.servicetitan.com/v1/docs/dispatch-pro-overview) · [Manage technicians with Dispatch Pro Auto Mode](https://help.servicetitan.com/docs/manage-technicians-with-dispatch-pro-auto-mode) · [Dispatch Pro FAQ](https://help.servicetitan.com/docs/dispatch-pro-faq) · [Titan Intelligence Overview](https://help.servicetitan.com/how-to/titan-intelligence-overview)

## 5 · Recurring jobs, capacity, and online booking

**Jobber.** **Recurring Jobs** carry a repeating schedule of visits — set from a "Repeating" dropdown when creating the job (every two weeks, first Monday of the month) — commonly used for weekly cleaning, snow removal, or monthly lawn care. Invoicing cadence is independent of visit cadence: per-visit, a fixed monthly price regardless of visit count, or a fully custom schedule (monthly on the last day, after each visit closes, or once the job closes). For inbound capacity, the **Client Hub** is the customer-facing portal where clients request work, approve quotes, view jobs, and pay invoices; **Online Booking** layered on top lets a client pick an actually-available slot, scoped to which team members' calendars are bookable and when, with Jobber auto-assigning whichever eligible member is free. Admins also control **Business Hours** (blocks closed periods from booking) and an "Earliest Availability" setting enforcing minimum lead time (e.g., no bookings within 2 business days) — lightweight-but-real capacity guardrails without a formal capacity-planning module.
Source: [Create a Recurring Job](https://help.getjobber.com/hc/en-us/articles/115009542848-Create-a-Recurring-Job) · [Online Booking](https://help.getjobber.com/hc/en-us/articles/13808363916951-Online-Booking) · [Business Hours](https://help.getjobber.com/hc/en-us/articles/360036391693-Business-Hours) · [Online Booking System for Field Services](https://www.getjobber.com/features/online-booking/)

**Housecall Pro.** **Recurring Jobs** support daily, weekly, monthly, yearly, and custom patterns; daily/weekly/monthly recurrences project a year out, yearly ones project five years out. Critically, each occurrence is its **own independent job record** (own invoice, own job number) rather than one job with many linked visits — the opposite of Jobber's model. That matters for reporting granularity: HCP's independent-job model makes each visit trivially reportable/invoiceable on its own, while Jobber keeps the whole series tied together by default. HCP further distinguishes **Appointments** (multiple visits sharing one job/invoice, for multi-day projects) from **Segments** (a job split into phases that each get their own schedule *and* invoice, used for phased installs). On booking, the **Online Booking** widget embeds on a business's website and integrates with **Reserve with Google**; set to "Auto-scheduling," bookings drop straight into the assigned employee's calendar, checking only that employee's real-time availability (plus Google Calendar sync) to avoid double-booking. For recurring **service plan** visits, the schedule page surfaces "optimal times" based on proximity to already-booked jobs to minimize drive time.
Source: [Create a Recurring Job](https://help.housecallpro.com/en/articles/2789168-create-a-recurring-job) · [Manage Recurring Jobs](https://help.housecallpro.com/en/articles/2845351-manage-recurring-jobs) · [Appointments vs. Segments vs. Recurring Jobs](https://help.housecallpro.com/en/articles/12892543-appointments-vs-segments-vs-recurring-jobs) · [Online Booking Overview](https://help.housecallpro.com/en/articles/7034474-new-online-booking) · [Service Plan Scheduling Suggestions](https://help.housecallpro.com/en/articles/8124459-service-plan-scheduling-suggestions)

**ServiceTitan.** The equivalent of recurring jobs is **Recurring Services**, living under **Memberships**/service agreements rather than as a calendar-native concept on their own — a customer signs a Membership (quarterly jetting, seasonal HVAC maintenance), and the platform automatically schedules recurring visits, handles customer communication, and equips the technician, without manual re-booking each cycle. Configuration uses a **Recurrence Type** (e.g., Monthly) and **Recurrence Interval** (e.g., 3 → every 3 months); a service can be continuous or a fixed visit count. For inbound booking, **Scheduling Pro** is the paid online-scheduling product (a step up from the older free "Web Appointment Scheduler") letting customers book via website or Reserve with Google, with data flowing directly into ServiceTitan. Its distinguishing feature is a choice of **four capacity models**: **Adaptive Capacity** (learns patterns from historical data, included in Core), **Adjustable Capacity Planning (ACP)** (pulls real-time shift data), **Business Hours** (unrestricted within set hours), and **Custom Capacity** (manual rules). Underpinning this is a genuine **capacity-planning layer**: help docs stress dispatchers need explicit capacity targets before booking starts ("without targets, dispatchers have no way to gauge whether a day is overbooked or underbooked"), every Job Type needs a nonzero Default Duration or "the capacity algorithm will massively overbook," and **Arrival Windows** (custom start/end ranges promised to the customer, per Business Unit) interact directly with both capacity math and Dispatch Pro's optimization flexibility — wider windows give Dispatch Pro more room to shuffle for revenue. Notably, Arrival Windows are explicitly **incompatible with Adaptive Capacity Planning**, since the latter depends on non-overlapping timeslots while arrival windows can overlap — a real architectural trade-off ServiceTitan documents itself.
Source: [Manage recurring services](https://help.servicetitan.com/docs/manage-recurring-services) · [Create and edit recurring service types](https://help.servicetitan.com/how-to/recurring-services) · [Scheduling Pro Home](https://help.servicetitan.com/docs/scheduling-pro) · [Plan Your Team's Capacity](https://help.servicetitan.com/docs/plan-your-team-s-capacity) · [Set up arrival windows](https://help.servicetitan.com/how-to/setup-arrival-windows)

## 6 · Segment differences: SMB vs. commercial/enterprise

The three products sit at genuinely different points on the complexity curve, and that shows up directly in scheduling/dispatch mechanics rather than just in marketing copy.

**Jobber** is priced and built for small-to-mid teams: four public plans — **Core** ($29/mo, 1 user), **Connect** (~$99/mo annual, 5 users), **Grow** (~$149–199/mo annual, 10 users), and **Plus** (~$399–599/mo depending on the source, 15 users) — with Route Optimization, GPS tracking, and Find a Time gated out of Core/Connect and only unlocked at Grow and above; Recurring Jobs unlock starting at Connect. There's no Business Unit-equivalent construct, no formal skills taxonomy, and no separate dispatch board — the calendar *is* the dispatch tool, a deliberate simplicity trade-off consistent with a self-serve, low-setup-time product.
Source: [Jobber Pricing](https://www.getjobber.com/pricing/) · [Jobber Pricing 2026: $29-$149/Mo Plans](https://fieldcamp.ai/reviews/jobber/)

**Housecall Pro** sits in between: **Basic** (~$59–79/mo), **Essentials** (~$149–189/mo, includes GPS tracking and QuickBooks sync), and **MAX** (~$299/mo, up to 8 users, advanced reporting and open API) — a solo operator on Basic reportedly *can't* track technicians on a map or sync QuickBooks, so even basic dispatch-adjacent capability (GPS) is paid-tier, similar to Jobber's gating pattern. HCP's own comparison pages position it against ServiceTitan as the faster-to-deploy, less operationally heavy option for roughly 1–50 technicians, conceding reporting depth to ServiceTitan for larger operations.
Source: [Housecall Pro Pricing & Plans](https://www.housecallpro.com/pricing/) · [Housecall Pro vs ServiceTitan](https://www.housecallpro.com/compare/housecall-pro-servicetitan/)

**ServiceTitan** is unambiguously built for the higher end: public commentary and ServiceTitan's own comparison pages describe it as designed for businesses running 20+ trucks with dedicated dispatchers and CSR staff, with all-in costs commonly cited around $500–$1,500+/month for a mid-size shop before add-ons, plus a real implementation project rather than instant self-serve setup. The scheduling/dispatch feature set reflects that: Business Units for segmenting residential vs. commercial work, a formal Skills system gating who can be assigned to a job type, Arrival Windows and multi-model Capacity Planning as first-class configuration, and both Dispatch Pro and Scheduling Pro sold as separate paid Pro add-ons requiring their own setup and — per ServiceTitan's own docs — meaningful historical data before the AI layer adds value. ServiceTitan's Contractor Playbook content also treats residential/commercial segmentation, multi-day construction crews (named roles like Foreman/Apprentice), and staffing capacity as distinct configuration problems, addressed at a depth neither competitor's help content reaches.

## What it means for FieldPulse

**The AI-dispatch race has three genuinely different starting points, not three copies of the same feature.** ServiceTitan's Dispatch Pro is a mature, revenue-optimization-framed auto-assignment engine with Auto/Assist modes and quantified (vendor-reported) ROI claims; Housecall Pro's CSR AI is squarely an intake/booking assistant that happens to also place jobs on the calendar; Jobber's AI investment (Receptionist + Find a Time) splits between call handling and slot-finding, with no publicly documented automated technician-assignment engine. Worth watching for FieldPulse's `#competitor-ai` monitor: if Jobber or Housecall Pro ever ships a Dispatch-Pro-equivalent (auto-assign on predicted job value/drive time/skill, not just "find an open slot"), that's a real category shift for the SMB tier, since today that capability is enterprise-exclusive and paid-add-on-gated even there.

**Skills-based assignment is a real, underexplored differentiator gap between the three.** ServiceTitan has a formal Skills object tied to Job Types that mechanically filters who's eligible for dispatch; Housecall Pro has informal tags its marketing describes as enabling skill-based assignment but without a documented structured taxonomy; Jobber appears to have no skills system at all — assignment is availability/location-driven only. In the SMB-to-mid-market zone where Jobber and Housecall Pro compete, "does the software know who's qualified for this job" could be a meaningfully different buying question than "is the software easy to set up."

**Recurring-job data modeling is architecturally different across all three, and it's not cosmetic.** Jobber keeps a recurring job as one job with many visits; Housecall Pro spins up an independent job record per occurrence; ServiceTitan ties recurrence to Memberships/agreements rather than treating it as a calendar-native primitive. This affects reporting, invoicing flexibility, and how "edit this one visit vs. edit the whole series" behaves — worth understanding before assuming any one mental model is the obvious one.

**Arrival windows and capacity planning are where ServiceTitan's enterprise orientation is most visible, and also where it creates real trade-offs.** The documented incompatibility between Arrival Windows and Adaptive Capacity Planning (overlapping windows vs. non-overlapping timeslot math) only surfaces once a platform tries to serve both promise-a-4-hour-window residential dispatch and precise multi-technician capacity math at once — a tension Jobber and Housecall Pro mostly avoid by not offering true capacity-model choice at all.

**"On my way" and live tracking have converged to table stakes, but the trigger mechanism differs.** All three vendors now do GPS-powered ETA texts with a trackable link, but Jobber's is manually triggered from the mobile app by a tech tapping a "how many minutes away" selector, while Housecall Pro's and ServiceTitan's are more automatic, system-triggered off dispatch status changes — a small but real UX difference for evaluating field-workflow friction.

## Questions this raises

- Does FieldPulse have (or need) a formal skills/qualifications object tied to job types, the way ServiceTitan does — or does staying in the "availability + location" model (Jobber's approach) actually serve FieldPulse's target segment better given lower setup overhead?
- Where does FieldPulse's recurring-job data model sit on the Jobber (one job, many visits) vs. Housecall Pro (independent job per occurrence) spectrum, and is that an intentional choice or an inherited one?
- Is there a FieldPulse equivalent to ServiceTitan's explicit capacity-target-setting workflow (Default Duration per job type, overbooking guardrails), or is capacity currently implicit/manual?
- Given that all three competitors gate meaningful scheduling capability (route optimization, GPS, AI assist) behind mid-to-top pricing tiers rather than entry plans, how does FieldPulse's plan structure compare — is core dispatch functionality available lower in FieldPulse's stack, and is that a deliberate wedge?
- If FieldPulse wants to compete on AI-assisted dispatch, which of the three postures is the right reference point: ServiceTitan's revenue-optimization auto-assign, Housecall Pro's booking-assistant-that-also-schedules, or something not yet offered by any of them at the SMB tier?
- Do FieldPulse's arrival-window/promise-time commitments (if any) interact with capacity math the way ServiceTitan's do, and has that trade-off been made explicit internally?
