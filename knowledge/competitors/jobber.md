# AI summary

Jobber has moved aggressively into AI for the SMB home-services segment, branding nearly everything as "Jobber AI" (with marketing/help docs still using legacy names like Copilot, Voice, Receptionist — naming is in flux). Its standout, genuinely differentiated bet is the AI Receptionist (calls AND texts, 24/7 booking, caller-ID match), a paid $99/mo add-on that is a real, shipped voice agent rather than a chatbot. The rest of the stack is solid-but-conventional embedded AI: a free beta in-product copilot for analytics/coaching, AI content generation in the Marketing Suite, AI-suggested automations, auto-drafted quotes ("Draft for me"), and AI-suggested review replies. The clear gap versus enterprise players is dispatch/scheduling, where Jobber remains heuristic route-optimization plus manual drag-and-drop dispatch with no true ML technician assignment. Overall posture: broad, well-packaged, SMB-pragmatic AI that leads on the customer-contact agent and is competitive-to-average elsewhere, with little true end-to-end agentic autonomy yet.
